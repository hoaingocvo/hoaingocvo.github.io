Reading — Make questions

Week 10

Hoai Ngoc Vo

Minor Threats by Mimi Thi Nguyen

Participants: Hoai, Kelem, Gabe, Nardos (a bit later than everyone)
Date & time: 4:00 p.m Wednesday Oct, 23 on Zoom

Questions

1. The author mentioned the concept of “minor objects”, what is it? How does it challenge or disrupt mainstream narratives about culture, race, and gender?
2. How does the author’s reference to “cruel optimism” connect to their understanding of punk’s potential disrupting societal promises, such as those of state or capital?
3. If we treat Archives as social formations as Ferguson suggests, how do we ensure that the archives maintain their original purpose, and are protected against bad actors?
4. If an Archive is to be seen as a social formation, how does one decide what perspectives or ideas are the best to view a topic from?
5. What is Mimi’s fascination with “punk” stem from, is it a sense of nostalgia or internal rebellion?
6. Why is black and brown punks different that a regular punk?
7. What are the potential pros and cons of embracing “minor threats” as a framework for activism?
8. Has “Minor Threats” by Mimi-Thi Nquyen affected activism today? And if so how?
9. What does the author mean by describing punk as both a “placeholder for institutionally improper craving” and a space immersed in “dumbfuckery”?
